Phase Transitions Using Monte Carlo Methods

The project investigates the beaviour of metal alloys using the Metropolis Monte-Carlo swapping alogrithm. A range of temperatures, compositions and interaction energies are simulated allowing for a quantitve description for the behviour of alloys at a range of temperatures.

Metropolis Monte Carlo Simulation for Physical Vapor Deposition

This project looks to model the growth of a thin film deposited by physical vapor desposition. The behviour of the particles, governed by thermodynamics is modelled using a Metropolis Monte Carlo algorithm to assess the impact of pressure, bonding energies and charge polarity on the overall coverage of a surface.

Simulation of Spinodal Decompoisition

This project solves the Cahn-Hilliard equation for a range of conditions to see the impact of the initial composition on spinodal decomposition.

Hopping Simulation of Diffusion

This project uses a Monte Carlo simulation to model diffusion behaviour, with diffusion being treated as the hopping of particles in one dimension.
